Design Systems

Building Accessible Typography Systems

By Jane Cooper • March 15, 2024 • 5 min read

Typography is the foundation of good design. A well-crafted type system provides consistency, improves readability, and enhances the overall user experience.

The Importance of Scale

A modular type scale ensures visual hierarchy and consistency throughout your design. Each size should serve a specific purpose, from small UI text to large display headings.

When choosing your scale, consider both readability and aesthetics. The base size should be comfortable for extended reading, typically between 16-18px for body text.

Responsive Typography

Modern web design requires typography that adapts to different screen sizes. Your type system should scale appropriately from mobile to desktop, ensuring readability across all devices.

Pro Tip

Use CSS custom properties (variables) to define your type scale. This makes it easy to adjust sizes globally and maintain consistency.

Accessibility Considerations

Accessible typography means ensuring sufficient color contrast, appropriate line heights, and readable font sizes. Follow WCAG guidelines to make your content accessible to all users, including those with visual impairments.

  • Maintain a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for body text
  • Use relative units (rem, em) instead of fixed pixels
  • Provide sufficient line height (1.5 or greater for body text)
  • Ensure text can be resized up to 200% without loss of functionality

Conclusion

A thoughtful typography system is an investment in your product's usability and brand identity. Take the time to build it right, and you'll reap the benefits in consistency, accessibility, and user satisfaction.
